The one thing your phrasing forces us to decide

You said "the spread … in terms of tendencies" — that's distributional. The engine has two ways to hold a style, and a trance profile probably wants both:

Constraints, or distributions?

Ruleset (constraints)

forbid / require / soft-prefer. Interpretable, citable, composes & specializes, feeds a generator as hard limits. But a soft-rule weight is a lossy projection of a distribution — it says "prefer X", not "X happens 62% of the time after Y".

Distribution (the spread)

degree-keyed transition matrices, IOI histograms, voicing-spread densities. This is the literal "spread" — you can sample it. gap 14 already scopes these as small versioned JSON priors, per-style.

Recommendation: a style profile is a named, versioned artifact that carries both — the induced ruleset (the interpretable, enforceable rules) and the distributions (the sampleable spread) — plus a provenance block (corpus? academic source? one artist's oeuvre?). That's the object that "lives among other derived rulesets"; it generalizes the named-ruleset library (gap D, just shipped) from single rulesets to whole styles. It's the genuinely new design — everything under it exists or is scoped.

"Living among other derived rulesets, academic ones, per-artist ones"

This part is already the architecture, not a wish. The named-ruleset library shipped this week; each entry is validated + provenance-stamped. Your three sources map to one field:

All three are the same object with different source/method provenance — the discipline already used for versioned priors and for wont's tag-contrast.1 stamps. A Fux ruleset and a Tiësto profile sit on the same shelf, honestly labeled by how each was derived.

So: are we aimed at that? Yes — it's the union of the induction track (4.6, shipped), the per-style distribution track (gap 14, scoped), the named library (gap D, shipped), and generative consumption (Phase 7 / the Wend-wont client pattern, proven). The trance case's specific blockers are gap B (harmony progressions — without it, "harmonies … progression tendencies" can't be expressed as rules yet), the distribution-valued prior (gap 14, to make "the spread" sampleable, not just soft-preferred), and the style-profile bundle (the new object above).

If this is the direction you want to commit to, the highest-leverage next build is gap B — it's the one hard blocker sitting between today and a trance harmony ruleset, it's already fully scoped in the ROADMAP, and it unblocks the harmony row for every future style.
