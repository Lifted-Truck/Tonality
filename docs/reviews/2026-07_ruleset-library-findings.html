The named ruleset library opens with Fux — and a map of what the DSL can't yet say

First-species counterpoint, hand-authored as a citable ruleset and validated through the same strict parser as any caller's. The five local rules are the complete, exact species law. But the point of authoring a real ruleset was never just the rules — it was to discover, precisely, where the DSL runs out of vocabulary.

The finding is the deliverable. gap D predicted that authoring the first ruleset would surface the DSL's expressiveness limits — "those hits are the deliverable, not a failure." It did: three distinct gaps, each now proven by a test that the rule cannot be authored. One of them is new.

The expressiveness map — three gaps, one of them new

First-species rules the DSL cannot express today. Each is proven real by a test, not asserted — the rule was attempted and the engine refused it or the field was shown absent.

NEW GAP cross-field

Compare two fields

"Counterpoint stays above the cantus firmus" is a_to_midi > b_to_midi. But a condition value is always a literal — never another field. No rule can relate two fields.

Where in the phrase

"Begin & end on a perfect consonance", "a single melodic climax" — all need a first / last / global position. No family carries one.

One empirical correction along the way: the schema docstring's example implied interval_class_to was a 0–6 interval class; probing the analyzer showed it's the vertical interval mod 12 (0 = octave, 7 = P5). The ruleset is authored against the real semantics, and the mod-12 reading is now documented.

Where this points: the cross-field-comparison gap is the cleanest, most reusable single addition (it would also unlock "this interval equals the previous", "the answer transposes the subject"…). Phrase/global scope and the pattern layer were already on the roadmap; this ruleset is now their concrete motivating evidence. Next library slices: second species (passing dissonance on weak beats — exercises the rhythm family), then a common-practice harmony set once gap B lands.
