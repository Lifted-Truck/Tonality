Rhythmic constraint search: it isn't an analogy — it's the same mathematics

Your question has an unusually clean answer. A rhythm cycle — onsets in N subdivision slots — is a bitmask over ZN, exactly as a pitch-class set is a bitmask over Z12. The engine's identity machinery (interval vectors, symmetry, DFT, prime forms, containment) isn't being borrowed for rhythm; it was never pitch-specific to begin with. The theory is mature: Toussaint's Geometry of Musical Rhythm, Amiot's Fourier treatment, and the maximal-evenness theorem all work in both domains simultaneously.

One mask, two musics

The proof by example: the mask {0,2,4,5,7,9,11} read as pitch is the diatonic scale. The same mask read as time is the bembé — the West African standard bell pattern, 7 strokes in 12 pulses. Same bitmask, same circular step pattern 2 2 1 2 2 2 1, same maximal evenness, same everything the engine computes.

Left: Z12 as pitch classes — C major. Right: the identical mask as 12 pulses of time — the bembé. Both are the unique maximally-even 7-in-12 pattern (Clough–Douthett); both have interval/IOI vector 2 5 4 3 6 1; both have df5 at its 7-note maximum. Every field the search layer ships today already describes both.

Three design realities (where it is NOT a free parameterization)

1 · Z_N, not Z_12 — the core is hard-coded to 12

Rhythm needs N = 8, 12, 16, 24… (straight vs compound subdivisions), but core/bitmask.py is mod-12 everywhere by doctrine. A rhythm search needs a small N-generic cyclic substrate — masks, rotation, IOI vectors, prime forms over ZN. Real work, not a parameter tweak.

1b · …which is Phase 6 arriving early (the bonus)

ROADMAP Phase 6 ("beyond 12-TET: generalized identity") needs exactly that ZN machinery for microtonal pitch. Building it for rhythm first means Phase 6 later reuses a tested cyclic core instead of greenfielding one. Rhythm is the low-risk proving ground: no enharmonics, no spelling, no priors.

2 · Rotation is NOT innocuous in rhythm — the granularity default flips

In pitch, transposing a set class is identity-preserving; in rhythm, rotation moves the downbeat — the tresillo rotated is a genuinely different groove. Meter anchors time the way key never anchors pc-sets. So Decision 12's sibling lands with opposite polarity: the rhythmic default should be rooted (slot 0 = downbeat), with necklace (rotation) and bracelet (+retrograde) folding as opt-in universes. Wend's brief-2 verdict — "rooted, always" for its generative queries — foreshadowed exactly this.

3 · It's the identity side only — durations live in realizations

An onset mask discards duration, velocity, micro-timing — deliberately. It is the rhythmic identity key; a performed rhythm is its realization (the reduction lattice generalizes cleanly). The realization side already exists: groove extract/apply, swing analysis, meter tracking. Search finds the pattern; groove tools dress it in time-feel. No overlap, clean seam.

Verdict & recommended handling

Sensible — strongly. Theoretically it is the identity layer's own mathematics on a different circle; the literature is mature and citable; the field vocabulary maps column-for-column; and the one real build cost (a ZN cyclic core) pre-pays Phase 6. Per the doctrine, the right move now is to record it as a gap with this design sketch (universe granularity flipped to rooted-default; identity-only scope; ZN substrate as the Phase 6 seam) and build when pulled — the likely first consumers being Wend (conditional/asymmetric meter, groove) and any drum-generator work. It should not jump the queue ahead of gap 19 (melodic tendency) without a consumer ask: gap 19 has measured evidence pulling it today.
