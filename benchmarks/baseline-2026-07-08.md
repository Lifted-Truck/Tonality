# Efficiency baseline — 2026-07-08 (first pass)

**Machine:** macOS 15.3.2, Apple Silicon (arm64), Python 3.13.1 · `main` @ the
gap-14-slice-1 merge. Numbers are machine-specific — a relative/regression
reference, not an absolute gate.

## Verdict

All three legs of the realtime-fitness contract are **green**. The engine is well
within interactive budgets (A3's soft-realtime target is 60–100 ms, pull-based;
the slowest single tool is **6.3 ms**), its pure layers are **freezable**, and its
contract artifacts are **sub-KB and load in microseconds**. No action required;
this is the reference to regress against.

## 1. Freezability — CLEAN ✅

No wall-clock reads or unseeded RNG in any pure layer
(`core, analysis, rules, temporal, search, representation, theory, context`). The
invariant that lets outputs freeze to deterministic contract artifacts holds by
construction. (The gap-14 `transition.py` sampler uses seeded `random.Random(seed)`
— correctly not flagged.)

> Recommend promoting `freezability.py` into `tests/` as a guaranteed invariant
> (it's deterministic and cheap) — a follow-on, per the audit-charter promotion path.
